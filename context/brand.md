# Brand — IntelliversePress

> First-pass starting point. David should confirm or redirect.

## Voice in three words

Clear, curious, approachable.

## Voice description

The voice of a smart friend who happens to know a lot about AI and is happy to explain it without making you feel small. Confident enough to teach, humble enough to admit complexity, never gatekeeping. Think: Scientific American or *The Economist*'s tech coverage at its best — accessible without being dumbed down.

## Things this brand says

- Plain-language explanations of technical concepts
- Honest takes on what AI can and can't do
- Curiosity-driven framings ("here's why this matters")
- Calls to think rather than buy

## Things this brand never says

- Hype-bait ("revolutionary," "game-changing," "10x your productivity")
- Doom-mongering about AI futures
- Jargon used as flex rather than tool
- Hard-sell language ("limited time," "act now")

## Visual direction

- **Mood:** Modern academic. Clean typography, generous whitespace, considered use of color.
- **Primary colors:** TBD — likely a deep blue or teal as accent against near-black text on warm-white background. Suggesting palettes for David: deep navy + warm cream + a single accent (orange or amber) for CTAs.
- **Typography:** Serif for body (warmth, "book-ness"), sans-serif for UI/headings. Free pairing suggestion: Source Serif 4 + Inter.
- **Imagery style:** Editorial photography or stylized illustration — not stock photos. Headshots professional but not corporate. Book covers as the visual anchor.
