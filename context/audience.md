## Primary audience: AI-curious learners

**Who they are:** Adults (25-55) who've heard about AI, used ChatGPT or similar, and want to actually understand how it works rather than just use it. Mix of technical professionals branching out, business decision-makers, students, and motivated general readers.

**What they want:** Clarity. They've read hot takes and watched videos and still feel like the floor under their understanding is shaky. They want a foundation.

**What they're worried about:** Falling behind. Being fooled. Buying a hyped book and finding it shallow.

**What they already know:** AI exists, ChatGPT exists, "neural network" is a phrase they've heard, things are moving fast.

**What they don't know:** How any of it actually works under the hood. Why it sometimes hallucinates. Whether the hype is real. How to think about AI in their own work.

**Vocabulary they use:** AI, ChatGPT, machine learning, neural network, model, training, prompt.

**Vocabulary that turns them off:** Buzzword stacking, breathless tech-bro tone, "thought leader" voice.

## Secondary audience: Industry professionals checking the book

**Who they are:** Engineers, data scientists, product managers who already work in or near AI and are evaluating whether to recommend or adopt the book for teams, students, or readers.

**What they want:** Signals of rigor without exhaustive academic citations. Quick read of the table of contents and a sample chapter.

## Where they come from

- Search ("AI fundamentals book," "best book to learn AI")
- Recommendations (word of mouth, professional networks, podcasts)
- Direct (reading the author's bio elsewhere and looking him up)
- Social posts about the book

## What they do next

The conversion action is **buy the book** — at whichever retailer they prefer. Secondary action: join a mailing list to hear about future titles.

## Audience anti-patterns

- AI doomers looking for fuel — not the audience, won't be served well
- Existing AI experts looking for cutting-edge research — not the audience; this is fundamentals, not novelty
- Pure thrill-seekers — won't enjoy the measured tone
