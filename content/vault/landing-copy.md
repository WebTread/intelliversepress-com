## Audience note

Target audience: Readers fairly new to technology and immersive book toolkits. Many will be arriving via QR code from the physical book for the first time. Copy should be **warm, reassuring, and provide gentle hand-holding throughout.** Assume visitors may be anxious about technology. Avoid jargon.

## 4. How to Use This Vault

_[Dev note: Helps orient new visitors. Consider placing in a visually distinct area or expandable/collapsible panel. Include callout graphic image where indicated.]_

**Section Header:** New here? Here's how this works.

Before diving into specific chapters, we encourage you to explore the vault a bit. Get familiar with the layout. Try an audio companion or watch a video overview to see what you're in for. Once you know what's here, you'll feel more confident returning as you progress through the book.

As you read AI Fundamentals, you'll notice a "Get the Most Out of This Chapter" callout near the beginning of each chapter. It looks like this:

Each callout includes a QR code and link that brings you directly to that chapter's bonus materials. You don't need to browse or search—just scan or click, and you'll land exactly where you need to be.

**Our suggestion:**

The vault is no substitute for reading the book—but many readers have found it helpful to preview unfamiliar topics here first. Listening to an audio companion or watching a video overview can give you a clearer sense of what's coming, making the chapter itself easier to absorb. Then, if you want deeper reinforcement afterward, you can return to the vault for another pass.

This approach works particularly well for concepts that feel new or outside your comfort zone. Let the vault warm you up, let the book go deep, and let the vault help it stick.

Take your time. There's no rush, no expiration, and no limit on how many times you can access these resources. They're yours.

### Audio Companions

Prefer to listen? You'll find a short audio companion for the entire book, plus one for each individual chapter. These walk you through the key concepts—perfect for commutes, walks, or reviewing before bed.

These aren't audiobook chapters; they're focused summaries designed to reinforce what you've read. If you find you learn best by listening to full narration, the complete AI Fundamentals audiobook is available on Audible.

One-page summaries you can save, print, or screenshot. When you need a fast refresher without flipping through the book, these have you covered.

## 8. Design Notes (from David)

- **Brand colors:** Reference the Intelliverse Press style guide. Callout boxes in the book use a **warm gold/cream palette with brown text.**
- **Tone:** Warm, supportive, patient. Assume visitors may be anxious about technology. Avoid jargon.
- **Mobile optimization:** Many visitors arrive via QR code scan on mobile. All elements must be touch-friendly and readable on small screens.
- **Email form:** Single field for email address, clear submit button, visible privacy assurance text.
- **Chapter navigation:** Visual grid or list. Each chapter link should feel inviting, not overwhelming.
